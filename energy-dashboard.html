Community-Driven Energy Solutions - Building on Local Strengths

Solar Energy for Critical Infrastructure
Strategic Integration with Project Activities:
  • WaSH Infrastructure (Activity 3.3): Solar-powered water pumps and treatment systems ensuring 24/7 operation
  • Early Warning Systems (Activity 1.2): Solar-powered communication hubs for women's platform messaging
  • Health Centers: Solar backup for vaccine storage and emergency medical equipment
  • Community Centers: Solar lighting for evening meetings and literacy programs
Burkina Faso Advantage: National solar capacity grew from 0 to 0.18 GW (2015-2023) - infrastructure and expertise available
Improved Biomass & Cooking Solutions
Building on Existing Community Practices:
  • Women-Led Training (Activity 2.1): Women first responders teach efficient cooking techniques
  • Cash-for-Work (Activity 2.2): IDPs and host communities build improved biomass stoves
  • Nutrition Programs: Efficient cooking reduces fuel costs, increases food budget
  • Health Co-Benefits: Reduced indoor air pollution improves respiratory health
Community Voice: "Traditional biomass stove + improved biomass stove" - EIA shows willingness to adopt efficient technologies

Strategic Advantage Through Evidence-Based Energy Management
Programmatic Benefits (Potential)
  • Risk Prevention: Solar backup systems would ensure critical infrastructure operates during grid failures
  • Cost Reduction: Improved cooking efficiency could reduce household fuel expenses by 30-50%
  • Health Integration: Efficient cooking technologies would reduce indoor air pollution, supporting health outcomes
  • Women's Empowerment: Energy-efficient technologies could reduce time burden for fuel collection and cooking
  • Climate Resilience: Reduced dependence on unreliable grid and fossil fuels
Field Impact (Potential)
  • Emergency Response: Reliable power systems would ensure early warning systems function during crises
  • Water Security: Solar-powered water systems could provide consistent access for 20,000 households
  • Health Protection: Improved cooking practices would reduce respiratory illness in women and children
  • Economic Relief: Lower energy costs would free up household income for food and healthcare
  • Community Safety: Solar lighting installations could improve security around water points and community centers
Implementation Strategy (Proposed)
  • Phase 1 (Proposed): Transportation efficiency and route optimization based on EIA findings
  • Phase 2 (Potential): Solar systems for critical WaSH and communication infrastructure
  • Phase 3 (Planned): Improved cooking stove programs through women's platforms
  • Phase 4 (Future): Community energy management committees and maintenance protocols
  • Integration Points: Energy efficiency could be embedded in DRR (30%), food security (45%), and WaSH (25%) activities
Ground-Level Feasibility (Assessment)
  • National Capacity: Burkina Faso solar sector growing rapidly (0.18 GW installed) - infrastructure available
  • Community Readiness: Traditional and improved biomass stoves already accepted
  • Partner Expertise: ADRA Burkina Faso has infrastructure implementation experience
  • Local Resources: Solar equipment and biomass materials available locally
  • Proven Technology: Simple, robust solutions appropriate for humanitarian contexts
What EIA Can Prevent:
  • Critical infrastructure failures during grid outages
  • Continued health risks from inefficient cooking practices
  • Wasted resources on energy-intensive approaches
  • Missed opportunities to build energy resilience
  • Higher operational costs from fuel inefficiency
What EIA Can Enable:
  • Reliable energy systems for life-saving humanitarian operations
  • Community-appropriate technology solutions
  • Integration of energy efficiency across all project activities
  • Building on national renewable energy momentum
  • Clear pathway from emergency response to sustainable energy systems
Bottom Line: The EIA can transform energy management from an operational challenge into a strategic advantage. We now have evidence-based recommendations that can enable reliable operations, reduce costs, improve health outcomes, and build community resilience - all while being immediately implementable through existing project structures and national energy infrastructure in Burkina Faso.
