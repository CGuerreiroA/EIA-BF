Our Mission & Approach
Human life is at the heart of our mission, and delivering life-saving assistance remains our absolute priority. To ensure this aid truly supports people's well-being now and into the future, we embrace a sustainable approach. This includes utilizing voucher assistance to empower communities, implementing responsible waste management practices, and rehabilitating essential water points. These vital actions are fundamental to protecting the environment that directly sustains human life, health, and dignity.

Addressing Critical Waste Issues - Burkina Faso EIA Findings

Hazardous Waste Crisis (Critical)
URGENT
No hazardous waste management in place
EIA Finding: "Very harmful to handle with extreme caution"
  • Immediate Action: Train women first responders on hazardous waste safety
  • Activity 1.1 Integration: Include hazardous waste protocols in first responder training
  • Community Solution: Establish specialized containment points in 20,000 households
  • Women's Platform: SMS warnings about hazardous materials via Activity 1.2
Ground Reality: This directly threatens health of women-headed households and children
Construction Waste & Procurement Gaps
Critical
No material reuse/recycling during construction
EIA Gaps Identified:
  • WaSH Infrastructure (Activity 3.3): Integrate waste management into water point construction
  • Local Materials: Reuse aggregates, sand, gravel from local sources
  • Cash-for-Work (Activity 2.2): IDPs collect and sort construction materials
  • Environmental Criteria (Activity 2.3): Train suppliers on sustainable practices
What Works: 25-50% already practice some waste segregation - build on this!
Paper/Cardboard & Knowledge Gaps
Opportunity
Promote rational paper use & collection
Sustainable Procurement Training Needs:
  • Activity 2.3: Address "lack of knowledge on sustainable procurement"
  • Local Steering Committees: Oversee paper/cardboard collection programs
  • Nutrition Education (Activity 2.4): Use recycled materials for education sessions
  • Women's Platform: Share best practices on paper conservation
Local Insight: Communities ready for collective solutions - leverage this willingness

  • Child Safety: Prevents hazardous waste exposure in 20,000 households through specialized containment and first responder training
  • Women's Economic Power: Cash-for-work waste management creates immediate income for women-headed households
  • Community Health: Proper waste management at water points prevents waterborne diseases affecting vulnerable populations
  • Social Cohesion: Shared waste solutions reduce IDP-host community tensions through collaborative problem-solving
  • Food Security: Composting programs directly support nutrition gardens and food production
